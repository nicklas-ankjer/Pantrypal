**original_problem_statement**: The user wants to build a mobile application called Kitchen Counter to manage household inventory, recipes, shopping lists, and emergency supplies.

**PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS**:
-   **Core Functionality**: Track home stock, manage recipes, create shopping lists, and monitor emergency supplies.
-   **Navigation**: A bottom tab bar for Recipes, Home Stock, and Emergency Stock. A top-right icon for the Shopping List.
-   **Home Dashboard**: A central screen showing low stock alerts, expiring items, cookable recipes, and shopping list reminders.
-   **Recipes**: Users manage their own recipes. The app should show ingredient availability from the Home Stock. A Cook Recipe function deducts ingredients from stock. A What can I cook? feature suggests recipes based on available ingredients.
-   **Shopping List**: Items can be added manually or automatically from recipes. Users can check off items and move them all to Home Stock.
-   **Home Stock**: Tracks everyday items with safety stock levels. Includes a Quick Add feature. Items can be organized into user-defined locations/categories (e.g., Freezer, Pantry).
-   **Emergency Stock**: Tracks long-term supplies sorted by expiration date, with notifications for expiring items.
-   **Design**: A clean, minimalistic, kitchen counter aesthetic with a warm, neutral color palette.

**User's preferred language**: English

**what currently exists?**
A functional full-stack Expo application with a FastAPI backend and MongoDB database. Most of the core features have been implemented:
-   **Frontend**: A complete UI with a tab-based navigation system for Home, Recipes, Home Stock, and Emergency Stock. Modals are used for adding/editing items and viewing the shopping list. The UI is data-driven, reflecting the state of the backend.
-   **Backend**: CRUD APIs for all major entities (Home Stock, Emergency Stock, Recipes, Shopping List) are implemented and working.
-   **Features Implemented**:
    -   Adding, editing, and deleting items in Home Stock, Emergency Stock, and the Shopping List.
    -   Creating recipes.
    -   A What can I cook? feature.
    -   A dashboard that shows low-stock alerts and recipe suggestions.
    -   A category/location system for Home Stock, allowing users to organize items into collapsible sections and manage these locations.
    -   Autocomplete for the shopping list input, suggesting items from the existing home stock.
    -   Tapping on stock/shopping list items opens a modal to edit quantity.
